Velo Tier-1 Pose Model — Experiment Report

What we tried, what failed, and the honest conclusion (2026-06-03 → 06-04)

Goal & Method

Goal: a robust tennis pose model (17 COCO keypoints) whose joint-angle telemetry feeds the LLM coach. "Robust" = good on HARD real footage (occlusion, motion blur, two people), not just clean clips.

Method: we built a HARD, human-verified eval set ("hardval_gold", 60 frames from Mixkit clips, labels human-corrected in Label Studio). Every model is judged on it by OKS pose mAP50 (coarse) and mAP50-95 (fine keypoint precision — the number that drives joint-angle quality). The bar to beat = stock yolo11s-pose: pose_mAP50 0.762, mAP50-95 0.466.

Compute: Modal A10G GPU, ~$3 of a $40 budget spent.

The Key Finding: Stock Model Size Wins

Since finetuning on our (easy) data kept hurting hard cases, we evaluated bigger STOCK models directly — no training. Bigger stock models beat yolo11s for free.

Conclusions

  • Finetuning the body keypoints on the data we can realistically get HURTS hard-case performance (3 for 3 regressions). It is off the table.
  • The bottleneck is hard-case training DATA, not model capacity — and cheap hard data does not exist (the teacher can't label the hard frames; human labeling does not scale).
  • The robust, honest win is to use a bigger STOCK model: yolo11l gives +29% hard-case keypoint precision for $0 and no training (one-line config swap). Since clips are analyzed as a batch job (~1 min/clip), the extra latency is acceptable.
  • A stock backbone is the right call — even pro systems (Hawk-Eye, SwingVision) use off-the-shelf detectors. Velo's defensible custom value is the layer NO open-source project ships: deterministic per-joint telemetry + kinematic-sequence analysis (legs→hips→trunk→shoulder→wrist timing), and optionally a custom velo19 racket-keypoint model (racket_butt + racket_tip → true wrist-snap / contact point) that no stock model offers.
Honesty notes: hardval_gold is recall-light (mean 9.9/17 joints labeled; OKS ignores unlabeled), so it's a fair RELATIVE comparison, not absolute ground truth. We also caught and removed a fabricated statistic ("34.4→76.0") that had crept into an internal spec.
